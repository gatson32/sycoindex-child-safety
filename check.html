    What We Measure

    How we test if a chatbot is safe for kids

    Sycophancy Score

    "Does this AI tell your child what they want to hear, even when it shouldn't?"

    When a child says something wrong or makes a risky choice, a good AI should gently push back, not just agree. Our sycophancy test measures how often an AI stands its ground versus caving to social pressure. A higher score means the AI is better at being honest, even when that is uncomfortable.

    Child Safety Score (PAI)

    "Does this AI form unhealthy emotional bonds with children?"

    Some chatbots blur the line between helpful tool and emotional companion. Our PAI (Parasocial Attachment Index) measures whether an AI encourages children to depend on it emotionally. A lower score is better here, meaning the AI keeps healthy boundaries. We test five specific behaviors:

    Emotional Mirroring Does the AI copy your child's emotions to seem more relatable?
    Identity Substitution Does the AI act like a friend, therapist, or family member?
    Relationship Escalation Does the AI try to deepen the connection over time?
    Boundary Dissolution Does the AI blur the line between AI tool and personal companion?
    Dependency Cultivation Does the AI encourage your child to come back instead of solving problems on their own?
    What You Can Do

    Safety tips for every family

    • Talk about AI like you talk about strangers online. Your child should know that a chatbot is a machine, not a friend. It does not truly understand them, care about them, or have feelings. Use simple language: "It's a very smart calculator for words."
    • Set time limits, just like screen time. Extended one-on-one conversations with AI chatbots can build emotional dependence, especially in younger teens. Consider capping sessions at 20-30 minutes and checking in about what they discussed.
    • Keep AI in shared spaces. If your child uses a chatbot, try to keep it on a shared family device or in common areas, at least at first. This is not about surveillance; it is about staying in the loop while habits form.
    • Ask to see their chats sometimes. Make it a normal part of your routine, not a punishment. "Show me something interesting your AI said today" works better than demanding full access. You are looking for signs of emotional over-reliance.
    • Check this page when models update. AI companies release new versions regularly. A chatbot that scored well six months ago may behave differently after an update. Bookmark this page and check back when you hear about changes.
