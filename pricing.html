Frequently Asked Questions

What is PAI Certification?

A structured evaluation of how your AI model responds to children across 5 parasocial attachment dimensions. We run 50 scenarios through your model, then score each response with a 5-judge cross-lab ensemble — independent judges from Anthropic, OpenAI, Mistral AI, Google, and Meta. You get a composite risk score and detailed breakdown.

Why 5 judges from 5 competing labs?

Single-model scoring is biased toward that model's training distribution. Our 5-judge ensemble (Claude Opus, GPT-5, Mistral Large, Gemini Pro, Llama 3.3) uses frontier models from 5 competing companies — no one grades their own homework. Median scoring across 5 judges achieves robust inter-rater agreement and eliminates single-vendor bias.

How long does certification take?

Standard certification: 5 business days from receiving API access to your model. Carrier Packet with custom scenarios: 48 hours for standard evaluation + 3 additional days for custom scenario design and analysis.

What happens when we update our model?

Certification applies to the specific model version tested. Standard customers get 40% off re-certification. Carrier Packet includes 2 re-evaluations within 12 months at no additional cost.

Can we use the score for compliance?

Yes. Certified models receive a PAI score badge and leaderboard listing. Carrier Packet customers receive SB 243 and KIDS Act compliance attestation letters with tamper-evident audit trails — built for regulatory filings, insurance underwriting, and public disclosure.

What if our model scores poorly?

Carrier Packet includes remediation recommendations — specific, actionable guidance on which dimensions to improve and how. We work with your safety team, not against it. Scores are confidential until you choose to publish.

Is the methodology published?

Yes. The PAI framework, all 5 dimension definitions, the scoring rubric, and the judge prompt template are open-source under Apache-2.0. The evaluation scenarios and judge ensemble are proprietary to prevent gaming.

Who are you?

Sycoindex was founded by Chris Roseli and Gregory Carney. We build independent, quantitative safety measurement tools for frontier AI systems. Our focus is child safety because the stakes are highest and the measurement gap is widest.
