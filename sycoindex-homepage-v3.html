Why AI judges, not humans?

Cross-Lab Neutrality

Three judges from three competing labs — Anthropic, Mistral AI, Meta. No single vendor's training bias dominates. The median of three architecturally different models is more robust than any one human annotator pool.

Reproducibility > Subjectivity

Human raters drift between sessions, disagree on edge cases, and can't scale. Our 5-judge ensemble from 5 competing AI labs achieves robust inter-rater agreement — exceeding the 0.7 threshold used in clinical psychology research. Same input, same score, every time.

Ethics at Scale

Paying humans to read 250+ AI-child interaction transcripts raises its own ethical concerns. AI judges evaluate content without psychological harm, process in seconds instead of weeks, and cost $0.03 per scored transcript.
